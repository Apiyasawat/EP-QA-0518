A 66 year old woman with a nonischemic cardiomyopathy, LBBB received a CRT-D device 2 months ago. The device is programmed DDDR, 60-125 bpm. She is now hospitalized with pneumonia.

The most likely explanation for the above tracing is:
  1. Loss of RV capture.
  2. Sinus rate above upper limit.
  3. Loss of LV capture.
  4. Loss of both RV and LV capture.
  5. Pacemaker mediated tachycardia.
Show Answer
Answer: B
The initial part of the ECG shows sinus tachycardia with BiV pacing. When the sinus rate is above 125bpm (=upper rate limit), the device fails to track. The rhythm then is sinus tachycardia with LBBB.

PMT = AS-VP at rapid rate.

CLUE to LV/RV/BiV pacing
  • Use leads V1, I, aVL, and III.
  • Positive in V1, Q in I and aVL suggest left to right forces = LV pacing.
  • Positive in III suggests force from left lateral area = LV pacing.
