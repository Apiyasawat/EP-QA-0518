Event recorder prior to episode of syncope in a 45 y/o man treated with flecainide for AF is shown above.

What is the most likely rhythm diagnosis?
  1. AF with aberrancy.
  2. AF with WPW.
  3. Atrial flutter.
  4. Ventricular Tachycardia.
  5. Artifacts.
Show Answer
Answer: C
The tracing shows borderline wide complex tachycardia with flutter waves seen clearly at the beginning of the tracing (marked with arrows).



The tachycardia later changes to a more rapid one but with identical QRS morphology. The flutter waves remain visible at the same cycle length.

This is an example of 1:1 atrial flutter which is due to the effect of type I antiarrhythmic agent in the absence of effective AV node blockade.

AADs AV node blocking property ECG features prompting discontinuation
Flecainide None QRS>25% wider
Propafenone Minimal QRS>25% wider
Sotalol A lot QT>500ms, QT increases>60ms
Amiodarone moderate QRS>500ms
Dronedarone Moderate QRS>500ms
Ref: 2016 ACC Guidelines for AF and 2016 ESC Guidelines for AF.
